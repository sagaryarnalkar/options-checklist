How to read. One window, two sections sharing the same time axis. Top ~70%: 1-min candles + score 1–10 markers. Default score basis is Write + Buy: bullish flow = PE writing + CE buying, bearish = CE writing + PE buying (switch to "Writing only" for the original writers-only score). A marker appears only when the dominant flow exceeds the threshold — default is a rolling mean + 2σ over the trailing 60 minutes (current minute excluded), so what you see on replay equals what you'd have seen live; no hindsight. Score 1 = at threshold, 10 = ≥4× threshold. Green ▲ below = bullish dominant; Red ▼ above = bearish dominant. The faint green/red background shading is the regime: sign of the rolling 30-min net flow (all four flows). Bottom strip: stacked per-minute flow at the ATM band — above zero the bullish flows (light green = PE writing, deep green = CE buying), below zero the bearish flows (light red = CE writing, deep red = PE buying). Writing vs buying is inferred from the price direction of the OI change: OI↑ with price↓ = sellers aggressing (writing); OI↑ with price↑ = buyers aggressing (buying) — every contract has both a buyer and a seller, so this is an aggressor heuristic, not a fact. Dashed ±1σ/±2σ lines = today's mean ± SD of total flow per side. The side panel's fund-flow table mirrors the reference indicator: last-60-minute and day totals per flow. Aggregate flow only — OI change is anonymous.
